SRIRANGAM RANGANATHAR TEMPLE

Srirangam, Tiruchirappalli

About

One of India's most revered temples, the Ranganathaswamy Temple in Srirangam, Tiruchirapalli, Tamil Nadu, is devoted to Lord Ranganatha, a manifestation of Vishnu, and his spouse, Ranganayaki (Lakshmi).

Constructed in the traditional Tamil architectural style, it is honored in the Alvars' hymns and is the most prominent of Vishnu's 108 Divya Desams.

Srirangam has been a site of constant worship for centuries and is regarded as the largest operational temple complex in the world.

Over the years, its enormous structures have been enlarged and restored; the most recent addition is the enormous 73-meter gopuram, which was finished in 1987.

Over a million devotees flock to the temple, which celebrates a grand 21-day festival during the Tamil month of Margazhi in accordance with the Tenkalai tradition of Sri Vaishnavism.

Internationally renowned, it was awarded the UNESCO Asia-Pacific Award of Merit for heritage conservation in 2017 and is included on UNESCO's tentative World Heritage list.

Location and Travel Methods

By Road (Bus): Located around 15 km from the old Central Bus Stand, 19 km from Panjappur Integrated New Bus Terminal, and 4.5 km from Chathiram Bus Stand. Regular buses and auto facilities are available from Chathiram Bus Stand.

By Air: The nearest airport is Tiruchirappalli International Airport, ~17 km away.

By Rail: The nearest railway station is Srirangam Railway Station, just 1–1.5 km from the temple.
