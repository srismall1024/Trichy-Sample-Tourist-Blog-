History

From 300 B.C. ahead, Woraiyur, that is presently part of Tiruchirappalli, functioned as the Chola capital.
Ancient literature and archaeological data both corroborate this.
Even all over the Kalabhra interregnum (A.D. 300–575), there are literary documents that indicate Woraiyur remained ruled by the Cholas.
Later, when Mahendra Varma Pallava I took the throne in A.D. 590, he governed throughout Woraiyur, modern-day Tiruchirappalli, and surrounding territories.
According to the inscriptions, this area was ruled by either the Pallavas or the Pandyas until A.D. 880.

Impact of the Carnatic Wars:
The battles between British and French East India Companies in the middle of the 18th century marked the height of the city's strategic significance.
British Administrative Shift:
When the Carnatic kingdom was placed under British rule in 1801, the Trichinopoly District was established, with Trichy serving as its capital.
Municipal Development:
Originally founded as Trichinopoly in 1866, the municipality was joined by Srirangam in 1871 and Golden Rock in 1972 to form the current Trichy Municipal Corporation in 1994.
Industrialization:
Trichy's industrial identity was cemented when the Great Southern of India Railway Co. (founded 1853) and its successor, the South Indian Railway (1874), established significant workshops there.
Industrial Backbone:
Trichy is known as the "Energy Equipment and Fabrication Capital of India" because it is home to heavy industries such as BHEL, the Golden Rock Railway Workshop, and the Ordnance Factory, among others.
Tourism:
Trichy is a popular tourist destination, with a rich heritage and natural beauty, making it a popular destination for tourists.
Renowned Institutions:
With top-tier institutions like NIT Trichy, IIM Trichy, IIIT, NLU, and a Government Medical College, the city is a center for education.
Origins of NIT:
Founded in 1964 as a REC, NIT was designated an Institute of National Importance in 2007 by the NITSER Act.
Other Historical Sites:
Railway Heritage Centre, Kallanai Dam, Erumbeeswarar Temple, Vayalur Murugan Temple, and the Butterflies Conservatory.
Colonial-era Architecture:
Constructed between 1839 and 1841, St. Mary's Cathedral is a Gothic-Romanesque structure that is still a well-known Catholic landmark in the center of the city.
