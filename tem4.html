Vayalur Murugan Temple

Kumaravayalur, Tiruchirappali

About

The Vayalur Murugan Temple is a Hindu shrine devoted to Lord Muruga, son of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati, located in the village of Kumaravayalur, near Tiruchirapalli, Tamil Nadu.
Believed to have been established in the 9th century during the rule of the Medieval Cholas, this temple is maintained by the Tamil Nadu Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Department.
Although Lord Shiva (Adinathar) is the presiding deity, the temple is famous for its Murugan shrine, and it is associated with the saint Kirupanandha Variyar.

The temple, over 1200 years old, stands near the Uyyakondan River amidst scenic green fields.
Lord Murugan is enshrined behind the Shiva sannidhi, while Adinathar and his consort Adinayagi are worshipped in the main sanctum.
Notable shrines include Athi Nathar and Agni Theertham (Shakti Theertham), believed to have been created by Murugan using his Vel (spear).
The Vanni Maram near the Rajagopuram serves as the temple’s sacred tree, and the five-tiered gopuram adds to its architectural grandeur.

The 15th-century Tamil saint-poet Arunagirinathar, who composed the devotional hymns *Thirupugazh* praising Lord Murugan, is deeply connected to this temple.
After being saved from despair by Lord Murugan, Arunagirinathar visited Vayalur and sang soul-stirring verses, enhancing the temple’s spiritual significance.

Location and Travel Methods

By Road (Bus): Located ~10 km from Old Central Bus Stand, ~19 km from Panjappur Integrated Bus Terminal, and ~13 km from Chathiram Bus Terminal.
Frequent buses and autos are available from Chathiram and Old Central Bus Terminals.

By Air: The nearest airport is Tiruchirappalli Airport (~16 km).
By Rail: The nearest railway station is Tiruchirappalli Junction (~11 km).
