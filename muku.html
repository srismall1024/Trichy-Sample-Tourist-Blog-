Mukkombu Dam and Park

About Mukkombu Dam

Inspired by the historic Grand Anicut of Karikala Chola, Sir Arthur Cotton constructed the Upper Anaicut, also called Mukkombu, a regulator dam across the Kaveri River between 1836 and 1838.

The Kollidam branches off from the Cauvery at this location, which is roughly 2 km from Jeeyapuram and 18 km west of Tiruchirappalli.

The 685-meter-long dam is used for irrigation and is also a well-liked picnic area with a beautifully landscaped park.

Here, the river divides to create the island of Srirangam, which is tucked away in the lush Thanjavur delta.
