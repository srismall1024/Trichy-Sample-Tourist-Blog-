Ucchi Pillayar Temple - Malaikottai

Tiruchirappalli, Tamil Nadu

About

The Ucchi Pillayar Temple is a revered Hindu shrine dedicated to Lord Ganesha, located atop the famous Rock Fort (Malaikottai) in Tiruchirappalli, Tamil Nadu.

Dating back to the 7th century CE, this temple holds both mythological and historical importance. The towering rock fort rises sharply above the city and offers breathtaking views of Tiruchirappalli.

According to legend, this is the place where Lord Ganesha eluded King Vibhishana of Lanka after installing the idol of Lord Ranganatha at Srirangam.

As the story goes, Ravana’s brother Vibhishana was gifted the idol of Lord Ranganatha by Lord Rama. While traveling through Tiruchirappalli, he entrusted the idol to a cowherd boy (Lord Vinayaka in disguise).

Vinayaka placed the idol on the riverbank, making it immovable. Angered, Vibhishana chased him up the rock and struck him, leaving a scar on Vinayaka’s forehead — still believed to be visible today.

Ganesha then revealed himself, blessed Vibhishana, and declared that the idol would remain in Srirangam permanently.

Location and Travel Methods

By Road (Bus): ~6 km from Old Central Bus Stand, ~15 km from Panjappur Integrated New Bus Terminal, and ~2 km from Chathiram Bus Stand. Frequent bus and auto services are available, especially from Chathiram and Teppakulam entrance.

By Air: Nearest airport is Tiruchirappalli International Airport (~10 km).

By Rail: Nearest stations are Tiruchirappalli Junction and Srirangam (~5 km).
