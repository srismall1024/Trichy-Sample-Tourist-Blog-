Thiruvanaikovil - Arulmigu Jambukeswarar Temple

Thiruvanaikovil, Tiruchirappalli

About

A well-known shrine devoted to Lord Shiva, the Jambukeswarar Temple is situated in Thiruvanaikaval near Tiruchirappalli, Tamil Nadu.

Respected as one of the five Pancha Bhoota Sthalams, it represents the element of water (Neer), one of the five elements of nature.

In keeping with this depiction, the temple’s sanctum features an underground water spring that flows continuously, symbolizing its divine connection.

The temple is one of the 275 Paadal Petra Sthalams, glorified in the hymns of the Nayanmars (Tamil Saiva saints).

Numerous Chola-era inscriptions attest to the temple’s ancient legacy. Here, Lord Shiva is worshipped as Jambukeswarar alongside Goddess Akilandeswari, a revered form of Parvati.

Location and Travel Methods

By Road (Bus): Located ~13 km from Old Central Bus Stand, ~18 km from Panjappur Integrated New Bus Terminal, and ~4 km from Chathiram Bus Stand. Buses and autos are available frequently.

By Air: Nearest airport is Tiruchirappalli International Airport (~15 km).

By Rail: Nearest station is Srirangam Railway Station (~3 km).
