Anna Science Centre – Planetarium

About Anna Planetorium

The Anna Science Centre in Tiruchirappalli, established by the Government of Tamil Nadu and inaugurated on 10 June 1999, is dedicated to sparking curiosity and promoting scientific awareness among students and the general public.

The centre features a 75-seater planetarium with daily shows in both Tamil and English, a 3D theatre screening educational films, and an environment gallery that highlights ecosystems, biodiversity, and conservation.

It also includes unique attractions like the Evolution Park, spread over two acres, showcasing the story of life on Earth.

Facilities such as Vigyan Prasar, EDUSAT, and Try Science connect visitors with global science resources.

Regular sky-watch programs allow people to observe celestial events through telescopes, creating engaging experiences during eclipses and other phenomena.

An upcoming Innovation Hub, costing ₹2.25 crore, will further support hands-on learning and experimentation for school students, making the centre a vibrant hub for science education and exploration.
