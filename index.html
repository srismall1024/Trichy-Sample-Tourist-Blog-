About Trichy

Tiruchirappalli, lovingly known as Trichy, is a city where history breathes through every rock, shrine, and riverbank.

Once the proud capital of the great Chola empire (around 300 B.C.), the city later came under the influence of the Pallavas, Pandyas, Hoysalas, Nayaks, Mughals, French, and the British — each dynasty leaving behind layers of architectural wonder.

The Nayaks gifted the city its most iconic landmark: the majestic Rock Fort Temple, overlooking the city along with the serene Teppakulam tank.

Situated gracefully along the banks of the sacred Kaveri river, Trichy was a major battlefield during the Carnatic wars of the 18th century, shaping South Indian and British colonial history.

Today, Trichy blends ancient glory with growing modernity. Be it the divinity of Srirangam, the calmness of Kallanai, or the beauty of Butterfly Park, the city offers something delightful for every traveler.

Trichy isn’t just a place — it’s an experience.
