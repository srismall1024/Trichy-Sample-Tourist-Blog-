TRICHY BUTTERFLY PARK

The Tropical Butterfly Conservatory in Srirangam, located within the Upper Anaicut reserve forest, spans 25 acres in the Cauvery and Kollidam river basin.
Recognized as the largest butterfly park in Asia, it was announced in 2012 by Chief Minister J. Jayalalithaa with a budget of ₹7.35 crore and inaugurated in 2015, later costing ₹8.67 crore.
The park features landscaped lawns, a butterfly glasshouse, fountains, boating, playgrounds, sculptures, and a 1.2 km walkway filled with nectar plants to attract butterflies.
A striking blue tiger butterfly sculpture welcomes visitors at the entrance. The park also includes Nakshatravanam, a “star forest” with 27 tree species linked to birth stars and 12 linked to zodiac signs, encouraging people to grow plants associated with their astrological sign.
Designed for both recreation and education, the conservatory blends natural beauty with cultural symbolism, making it a unique attraction in Tamil Nadu.
